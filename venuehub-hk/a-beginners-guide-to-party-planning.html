A Beginner’s Guide to Party Planning

Planning a party or event can be a truly enjoyable experience, though there are certain factors that can complicate the process. There are a lot of things to consider when it comes to planning events and things can seem overwhelming for those who have limited planning experience. A beginner event planner might have a particular vision in their mind, but find it very challenging to put it all together and help the event to go off without a hitch.

Experienced planners know exactly what to do when things can seem to be getting out of control, and a lot of party planning does come down to managing things that will inevitably go awry. This can happen no matter how well an event has been planned, so planners should always be aware of the ways they can minimise potential risks to their plans.

Be flexible

It is easy to get hung up on an idea, but inflexibility can lead to disaster. If you can try to be flexible while planning a party or event then you will be better placed to deal with any curve balls that might be sent your way. Be flexible when choosing an event space or date for your event, and keep your options open in terms of catering, decorations and entertainment options.

Set a budget and stick to it

Any experienced planner will tell you than setting a budget and sticking to it as closely as possible helps enormously when putting an event together. It can be so easy to face spiralling costs, even if you have a large budget, and this can cause trouble between planners and clients. Resisting the urge to overspend can be hard, but remember that you will need to retain some of your budget to account for unexpected costs and fees along the way.

At times, your first choice of food supplier or entertainment might fall through, meaning you will likely need to find a replacement at relatively short notice. Often this can lead to more being spent on a particular aspect of the event that you previously had intended, so giving yourself a buffer within your budget can help reduce stress and friction if this should happen.

Fit the venue to the event

There are such a huge range of venues available for your party planning needs that narrowing your options early on should be a priority. Choose venues which suit the event such as specialist wedding room hire which matches the theme of the wedding being planned. Spend some time researching venues and make sure you have a good feel for a place’s vibe before you put down any deposits.

Instagram, Pinterest and other social media platforms are great places to see other events that have taken place at a venue, giving you a clearer idea of how that venue will work with your planned party. If possible, try to visit each potential venue before you make your decision as this will give you a good idea of how to plan other elements of the event so they are a good fit for the space.

Choosing a date

Due to the average work schedule, most big events and parties take place on either Fridays, Saturdays or Sundays so that attendance doesn’t interfere with most people’s working life. This does mean that some venues will put their prices up for these days of the week, so if you have the ability to do so you can often save some money by throwing your event during the week.

If you are planning an event for a holiday then you may need to book your party venue hire well in advance as there is likely to be more competition for venues during these peak times of the year. Generally speaking, summer is a peak time for parties so this can also narrow your venue choices unless you plan well in advance.

Whichever date you choose, be sure to check your venue has availability for that day before you send out your invites. Once the venue has given confirmation then let your guest list know the party details as soon as possible and make sure they know how to RSVP and when this needs to be done by.
